Project

Chartist walking trials (Nov 2019 – Jan 2020)

The project was given to us by the Newport City Council. It consisted of creating a website application to help the users in finding the easiest walking routes. The project was developed in accordance with the client's requirements which was to display the Chartist Trail in Newport with directions, heritage orientated information, feedback page, and an admin console whereby the client could edit the Chartist Trail. The website application consisted of two user types, the admin and the trail walker. In our team, we used Taiga as our form of agile scrum methodology. This ensured we had regular meetings to discuss previous, current, and future tasks for the project and track progress of teammates. Also, we used tools such as: Git, Taiga, Flask, Python, MySQL, HTML/CSS, Ajax, JavaScript, jQuery, Geolocation, External Libraries e.g. Bootstrap, Cookies, and Sessions.

VOLUNTEERING

Red Cross youth, Denmark (2014-2018)

I was the Head of Linguistics and part of a bigger team devoted towards empowering young refugees. My role, being trilingual, involved teaching them Danish and English languages through taking them out, mentoring them, as well as well as encouraging them to share their experiences. This proved immensely challenging, at first, given how most of them did not either speak English or were not too vocal after their tragic experiences in Syria. However, through my patience and repetitive hard work, I managed to help a group of 50 young refugees surmount the immense language barrier.
